## SCORING METHODOLOGY

### Total Score: 25 points maximum
Each criterion rated 1-5, total possible score = 25

### Scoring Criteria Breakdown

#### 1. COMPANY SIZE (1-5 points)
Measures revenue, market presence, and ability to pay

**5 points - Enterprise Giant**
- Revenue: $500M+ USD annually
- Employees: 10,000+
- Examples: Mercado Libre, Claro, Itaú Unibanco, LATAM Airlines
- Contract potential: $3,997+/month (Enterprise tier)

**4 points - Large Enterprise**
- Revenue: $100M-500M USD
- Employees: 1,000-10,000
- Examples: Magazine Luiza, iFood, Rappi, Nubank
- Contract potential: $1,497-3,997/month (Professional to Enterprise)

**3 points - Mid-Market**
- Revenue: $20M-100M USD
- Employees: 200-1,000
- Examples: Dafiti, Netshoes, ClickBus
- Contract potential: $1,497/month (Professional tier)

**2 points - Growing SMB**
- Revenue: $5M-20M USD
- Employees: 50-200
- Examples: Regional hotel chains, smaller retail brands
- Contract potential: $497-1,497/month (Starter to Professional)

**1 point - Small Business**
- Revenue: <$5M USD
- Employees: <50
- Contract potential: $497/month (Starter tier)
- Note: Likely too small for initial focus

---

#### 2. SOCIAL MEDIA ACTIVITY (1-5 points)
Measures follower count, engagement rate, comment volume

**5 points - Massive Presence**
- Instagram: 3M+ followers
- Facebook: 5M+ followers
- Comment volume: 5,000+ per month
- Engagement rate: High (>2%)
- Examples: Mercado Libre (15M+), Magazine Luiza (8.5M), McDonald's LATAM

**4 points - Very Active**
- Instagram: 1M-3M followers
- Facebook: 2M-5M followers
- Comment volume: 2,000-5,000 per month
- Engagement rate: Medium-High (1-2%)
- Examples: iFood (3.5M), Nubank (3.5M), Claro (2M)

**3 points - Active**
- Instagram: 500K-1M followers
- Facebook: 1M-2M followers
- Comment volume: 500-2,000 per month
- Engagement rate: Medium (0.5-1%)
- Examples: Dafiti (2.8M combined), Entel, Inter

**2 points - Moderate**
- Instagram: 100K-500K followers
- Facebook: 500K-1M followers
- Comment volume: 200-500 per month
- Examples: Regional brands, smaller chains

**1 point - Low Activity**
- Instagram: <100K followers
- Comment volume: <200 per month
- Note: May not justify platform cost

---

#### 3. APPARENT NEED (1-5 points)
Measures visible pain points, complaint volume, negative sentiment

**5 points - Critical Need (Crisis)**
- Negative sentiment: 60-80%
- Visible reputation crisis
- High churn risk mentions
- Public complaints from customers
- Examples: Claro Brasil (1.5★ Trustpilot), Oi Telecom
- **Value Proposition**: Reputation recovery, crisis prevention

**4 points - High Need**
- Negative sentiment: 40-60%
- Frequent complaints visible
- Multiple pain point categories
- Customer service overwhelmed
- Examples: Gol Airlines, Avianca (flight complaints), Coppel (credit issues)
- **Value Proposition**: Reduce complaint volume, improve response time

**3 points - Moderate Need**
- Negative sentiment: 20-40%
- Manageable but growing complaints
- Specific pain points (delivery, billing, etc.)
- Examples: Mercado Libre (seller disputes), iFood (delivery issues)
- **Value Proposition**: Proactive issue detection, competitive advantage

**2 points - Low Need**
- Negative sentiment: 10-20%
- Few visible complaints
- Generally positive brand
- Examples: Nubank (strong community), Natura (brand loyalty)
- **Value Proposition**: Maintain competitive advantage, protect brand equity

**1 point - Minimal Need**
- Negative sentiment: <10%
- Very strong brand reputation
- Note: Hard to demonstrate ROI

---

#### 4. BUDGET LIKELIHOOD (1-5 points)
Measures ability and willingness to pay for B2B SaaS

**5 points - High Budget & Tech-Savvy**
- Industry: Fintech, Digital-first, E-commerce platforms
- Proven SaaS spend: Multiple platforms visible
- Decision speed: Fast (30-60 days)
- Examples: Nubank, iFood, Mercado Libre, Magazine Luiza
- **Why**: Already investing in AI/ML, data analytics, digital transformation

**4 points - Corporate Budget Available**
- Industry: Large retail, banking, telecom
- Proven SaaS spend: Some platforms
- Decision speed: Medium (60-90 days)
- Examples: Itaú, Bradesco, Vivo, Falabella
- **Why**: Budget exists but procurement process slower

**3 points - Budget Possible**
- Industry: Traditional retail, hospitality, automotive
- SaaS adoption: Growing
- Decision speed: Slower (90-120 days)
- Examples: Liverpool, C&A, NH Hoteles
- **Why**: Budget available but requires business case approval

**2 points - Limited Budget**
- Industry: Utilities, regional chains
- SaaS adoption: Low
- Decision speed: Very slow (120+ days)
- Examples: AES Eletropaulo, Comgás, regional supermarkets
- **Why**: Budget constrained, traditional procurement

**1 point - Unlikely Budget**
- Very cost-sensitive industry
- Limited SaaS adoption
- Note: Requires strong ROI proof

---

#### 5. ACCESSIBILITY (1-5 points)
Measures ease of reaching decision-makers

**5 points - Highly Accessible**
- LinkedIn: Decision-makers active on LinkedIn
- Company culture: Known for innovation, open to new tools
- Contact availability: Easy to find (LinkedIn, company site)
- Referral potential: Warm intro possible
- Examples: Nubank (CEO active on LinkedIn), Magazine Luiza (CEO accessible)
- **Approach**: Direct LinkedIn message, personalized email

**4 points - Accessible**
- LinkedIn: Decision-makers present on LinkedIn
- Company culture: Open to B2B partnerships
- Contact availability: Can be found with research
- Examples: iFood, Rappi, Mercado Libre
- **Approach**: LinkedIn + personalized email + free report

**3 points - Moderately Accessible**
- LinkedIn: Some decision-makers on LinkedIn
- Company culture: Traditional but digitizing
- Contact availability: Requires deeper research
- Examples: Banks (Itaú, Bradesco), Telecom (Vivo, Claro)
- **Approach**: Multi-touch campaign, may need referral

**2 points - Hard to Reach**
- LinkedIn: Limited presence
- Company culture: Very traditional, hierarchical
- Contact availability: Gatekeepers present
- Examples: Government-owned (Banco do Brasil, Caixa)
- **Approach**: Requires warm intro, event networking

**1 point - Very Difficult**
- International HQ, regional offices hard to navigate
- Company culture: Closed to cold outreach
- Note: Save for later, focus on easier wins first
