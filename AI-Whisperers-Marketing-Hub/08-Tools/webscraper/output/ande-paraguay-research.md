# ANDE (Administración Nacional de Electricidad) - Research Report

## Company Overview

**ANDE** is Paraguay's National Electricity Administration (*Administración Nacional de Electricidad*), a state-owned autonomous public entity with legal personality responsible for operating the country's electrical transmission, distribution, and generation systems. It holds a monopoly on electrical services across much of Paraguay's national territory.

- **Type**: State-owned autonomous entity
- **Employees**: Over 4,900 staff members (one of Paraguay's largest public institutions)

## Historical Background

### Origins (1910-1948)
- **November 28, 1910**: Parliament authorized Italian entrepreneur Juan Carosio to operate an electric tram network and power plant for public lighting
- **1913**: The company was acquired by a British company
- **1919**: Carosio reacquired the rights as head of the American Light and Traction Company

### Nationalization
- **August 11, 1948**: Law No. 16 nationalized electricity and electric transport services
- **October 14, 1948**: Renamed as National Electricity and Tramway Company
- **November 22, 1948**: Resolution No. 478 officially established the name "Administración Nacional de Electricidad (ANDE)"
- **March 29, 1949**: ANDE created as an autonomous state entity with legal personality
- **August 12, 1964**: Law No. 966 formally created ANDE and established its organic charter

## Mission and Services

**Mission**: To satisfy the country's electrical energy needs and operate in the Regional Electrical Sector with Socioenvironmental Responsibility, contributing to Paraguay's development and the welfare of its population.

### Core Services
- **Electricity Generation**: Power production facilities
- **Transmission**: High-voltage power transmission network
- **Distribution**: Electrical distribution to customers nationwide

### Customer Services
- "Mi Ande" portal for online account management
- Virtual consultation center
- Multiple local payment locations
- Meter reading services
- Complaint systems for outages and commercial disputes
- Energy theft reporting under Law 7300

## Recent Achievements and Developments

### Financial Performance
- **7th consecutive year** recognized as the largest contributor to Paraguay's treasury in 2024
- **Net tax contribution**: G. 729,685 million in 2024
- **Energy consumption growth**: 18% increase between 2023 and 2024 (global record)

### Operational Improvements
- **49% reduction** in electrical outages during summer 2024-2025
- Development of a master plan to strengthen the electrical system

### Strategic Planning
- Proposed five strategic pathways for Paraguay's electrical future through 2043
- Plans include increased renewable energy integration and private sector participation

### Workforce Expansion
- Announced incorporation of 140 staff members and 177 apprentices in late 2024
