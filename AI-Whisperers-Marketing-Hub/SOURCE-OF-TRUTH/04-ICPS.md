## ICP 1: Solo Entrepreneur / Solopreneur

### Demographics
| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Age | 28-45 |
| Location | Global (English-speaking) |
| Role | Founder, freelancer, consultant, creator |
| Company Size | Self-employed or 1-2 people |
| Revenue | $50K - $150K/year |
| Tech Savviness | Medium to High |

### Budget & Services
- **Budget Range**: $500 - $5,000
- **Best Services**:
  - Web design ($50/hr) — 10-100 hours = $500-$5,000
  - Consulting ($100/session) — affordable entry point
  - Training (course fee) — self-paced learning
- **Payment**: Often upfront, credit card

### Key Pain Points
1. Time scarcity - wearing too many hats
2. Limited budget - can't afford full team
3. Knowledge gap - overwhelmed by AI options
4. Fear of complexity - worried it's too technical
5. FOMO - competitors using AI

### What They Want
- More time
- Scale without hiring
- Simple solutions
- Quick wins

### Buying Triggers
- Sees case study with clear ROI
- Free trial or low-risk entry
- Step-by-step support
- Results in days, not months

### Where They Hang Out
- LinkedIn
- Twitter/X
- YouTube
- Reddit (r/Entrepreneur)
- Indie Hackers

### Key Messages That Work
- "Get your time back"
- "Scale without hiring"
- "It will work. And it won't take forever."

## ICP 2: SME Operations Manager

### Demographics
| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Age | 30-50 |
| Location | Global |
| Role | Ops Manager, COO, Operations Director |
| Company Size | 10-200 employees |
| Revenue | $1M - $50M/year |
| Tech Savviness | Medium |

### Budget & Services
- **Budget Range**: $5,000 - $50,000
- **Best Services**:
  - AI automation (fixed price) — automate repetitive processes
  - Custom microservices ($80/hr) — 60-600 hours = $5K-$50K
  - Web design ($50/hr) — company websites, internal tools
- **Payment**: Net 30, sometimes PO required

### Key Pain Points
1. Team stretched thin
2. Manual processes causing errors
3. Can't hire fast enough
4. CEO pressure to do more with less
5. Tech debt from cobbled solutions

### What They Want
- Efficiency gains
- Error reduction
- Scalable processes
- Team productivity

### Buying Triggers
- Cost reduction data
- Case study from similar company
- Quick implementation timeline
- Team training included

### Where They Hang Out
- LinkedIn
- Industry conferences
- Peer networks
- Operations communities

### Key Messages That Work
- "Do more with less"
- "If you have to do it more than once, automate it"
- "It will work. And it won't take forever."

## ICP 3: Corporate Innovation Lead

### Demographics
| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Age | 35-55 |
| Location | Global |
| Role | Innovation Lead, Digital Transformation, VP Operations |
| Company Size | 200+ employees |
| Revenue | $50M+ |
| Tech Savviness | High (conceptually) |

### Budget & Services
- **Budget Range**: $25,000 - $500,000
- **Best Services**:
  - Custom microservices ($80/hr) — large-scale projects
  - AI automation (fixed price) — department/company-wide
  - Consulting ($100/session) — strategy and planning
- **Payment**: Net 60-90, formal procurement

### Key Pain Points
1. Need to prove AI ROI to leadership
2. Long procurement cycles
3. Integration with legacy systems
4. Change management challenges
5. Risk aversion in organization

### What They Want
- Proven pilot results
- Scalable solutions
- Executive-ready reporting
- Low-risk implementation

### Buying Triggers
- Board/CEO mandate for AI
- Competitor doing AI well
- Budget available for innovation
- Need quick win to justify larger investment

### Where They Hang Out
- LinkedIn
- Industry conferences
- Executive networks
- Analyst reports

### Key Messages That Work
- "Prove AI ROI with a pilot project"
- "From pilot to scale"
- "It will work. And it won't take forever."

## Negative Personas (Who NOT to Target)

### Don't Pursue If:
- Revenue under $30K/year (can't afford)
- Wants "someone to handle everything" (needs employee, not automation)
- Expects magic with zero input
- Has tried 5+ solutions and blamed all of them
- Unwilling to share data or access

### Warning Signs in Conversations:
- "Just handle everything for me"
- "I can't spend ANY time on this"
- "My business is completely unique"
- "Nothing has ever worked for us"
- Won't provide access to systems
