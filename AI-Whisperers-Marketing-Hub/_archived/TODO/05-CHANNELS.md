# LinkedIn Daily Playbook

> Execute this every day to build your LinkedIn presence and pipeline

---

## Daily Schedule (45-60 min total)

### Morning Block (20 min) - 8:00-8:20 AM

#### 1. Check Notifications (5 min)
- [ ] Respond to comments on your posts
- [ ] Reply to DMs
- [ ] Accept/review connection requests

#### 2. Engage on Feed (10 min)
- [ ] Comment on 5 posts from target audience
- [ ] Comment on 3 posts from industry leaders
- [ ] Share/react to 2 relevant posts

**Comment Guidelines**:
- Add value, don't just agree
- Ask thoughtful questions
- Share relevant experience
- Minimum 3 sentences per comment

#### 3. Send Connection Requests (5 min)
- [ ] Send 10 targeted connection requests
- [ ] Use personalized notes (not default)

**Connection Request Template**:
"Hi [Name], I noticed [specific observation about their profile/content]. I help [their role type] automate [relevant task]. Would love to connect!"

---

### Midday Block (15 min) - 12:00-12:15 PM

#### 4. Respond to Morning Engagement (10 min)
- [ ] Reply to comments on your post
- [ ] Continue conversations in DMs
- [ ] Send follow-up messages to warm connections

#### 5. Content Check (5 min)
- [ ] Review post performance
- [ ] Note what's resonating
- [ ] Adjust tomorrow's content if needed

---

### Afternoon Block (20 min) - 4:00-4:20 PM

#### 6. Outreach (15 min)
- [ ] Send 5 new outreach messages
- [ ] Follow up on 5 previous conversations
- [ ] Move warm leads to email/call

**Outreach Message Structure**:
1. Personal observation (shows you did research)
2. Problem they likely face (based on role/industry)
3. Hint of solution (without pitching)
4. Soft CTA (question, not ask)

#### 7. Tomorrow's Content (5 min)
- [ ] Draft or schedule tomorrow's post
- [ ] Queue any scheduled content
- [ ] Note any content ideas from today

---

## Weekly Rhythm

### Monday: Plan
- Review last week's analytics
- Set this week's content themes
- Identify 20 target profiles to engage with

### Tuesday-Thursday: Execute
- Follow daily schedule
- Post your best content mid-week
- Heavy outreach days

### Friday: Analyze & Prepare
- Download weekly analytics
- Identify top-performing content
- Prepare weekend content (if posting)
- Set up next week

---

## Content Posting Schedule

### Optimal Times:
- **Best**: Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10 AM
- **Good**: Monday 8-10 AM, Friday 8-10 AM
- **Avoid**: Weekends, after 6 PM

### Posting Frequency:
- **Minimum**: 3 posts/week
- **Ideal**: 5 posts/week (weekdays)
- **Maximum**: 1-2 posts/day

### Content Mix Per Week:
- 2 educational posts (tips, how-to)
- 1 story post (personal experience)
- 1 social proof post (results, testimonials)
- 1 engagement post (question, poll)

# Email Marketing Playbook

> Build and nurture your email list

---

## Email Types

### 1. Newsletter (Weekly)
**Purpose**: Stay top of mind, provide value
**Frequency**: Every Tuesday, 9 AM
**Length**: 300-500 words

**Structure**:
- 1 main insight/lesson
- 1 quick tip
- 1 resource or tool
- 1 CTA (soft)

### 2. Nurture Sequences (Automated)
**Purpose**: Move leads through funnel
**Trigger**: Lead magnet download, form fill
**See**: `05-Sales/email-sequences/`

### 3. Promotional (Occasional)
**Purpose**: Drive specific action
**Frequency**: Max 2/month
**When**: New offer, limited availability, case study

---

## Newsletter Calendar

### Monthly Theme Rotation:
- Week 1: Educational (how-to)
- Week 2: Case study / results
- Week 3: Tool/resource share
- Week 4: Behind-the-scenes / personal

### Content Sources:
- LinkedIn posts that performed well (expand)
- Client wins and learnings
- Industry news + our take
- Tools we're using

---

## List Segmentation

### Segments:
1. **By ICP**
   - Solopreneurs
   - SME Ops Managers
   - Corporate Innovation

2. **By Stage**
   - Subscriber (newsletter only)
   - Lead (downloaded lead magnet)
   - SQL (had discovery call)
   - Customer (bought something)

3. **By Engagement**
   - Active (opened in last 30 days)
   - Cooling (no opens in 30-60 days)
   - Cold (no opens in 60+ days)

### Segment-Specific Content:
- Solopreneurs: Focus on time savings, solo tools
- SME Ops: Focus on team efficiency, cost reduction
- Corporate: Focus on ROI, pilot programs, scale

---

## Subject Line Framework

### Formulas That Work:
- **Question**: "Are you still doing [task] manually?"
- **How-to**: "How to [achieve outcome] in [timeframe]"
- **Number**: "3 automations that save 10 hours/week"
- **Curiosity**: "The automation mistake most people make"
- **Personal**: "A lesson from my worst client call"
- **Results**: "How [client] saved [X hours/week]"

### A/B Test Plan:
- Test 2 subject lines per send
- Minimum list size for test: 1,000
- Winner criteria: Open rate after 4 hours
- Document results in test log

---

## Writing Guidelines

### Tone:
- Conversational, not corporate
- First person ("I" not "We" for personal brand)
- Direct and concise
- Occasional humor okay

### Structure:
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Use bullet points and white space
- One clear CTA per email
- Mobile-friendly (most opens are mobile)

### CTA Options:
- "Reply to this email"
- "Read the full post [link]"
- "Book a call [link]"
- "Download the guide [link]"
- "Watch the video [link]"

---

## List Building Tactics

### Active:
- Lead magnet on website
- LinkedIn profile CTA
- Content upgrades on blog posts
- Webinar registrations

### Passive:
- Email signature CTA
- End of blog posts

### Growth Target:
