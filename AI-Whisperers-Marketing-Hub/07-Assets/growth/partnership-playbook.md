# Partnership Playbook

> Strategic alliances that grow both businesses

---

## What is a Partnership?

A formal relationship with a complementary business where you:
- Refer business to each other
- Co-create content or offers
- Access each other's audiences
- Potentially share revenue

**Different from referrals**: Partnerships are ongoing relationships, not one-time introductions.

---

## Types of Partnerships

### 1. Referral Partnerships
**How it works**: You refer clients to them, they refer clients to you
**Best for**: Complementary service providers
**Example**: We partner with a CRM consultant - we automate, they implement

### 2. Reseller Partnerships
**How it works**: Partner sells your service, earns commission
**Best for**: Partners with sales capacity
**Example**: A digital agency includes our automation as part of their offering

### 3. Integration Partnerships
**How it works**: Your services technically connect
**Best for**: Software/tool providers
**Example**: We implement using their platform, we're listed as certified partner

### 4. Co-Marketing Partnerships
**How it works**: Create content or events together
**Best for**: Non-competing businesses with similar audiences
**Example**: Joint webinar on "AI + CRM: Better Together"

### 5. Strategic Alliances
**How it works**: Deep collaboration on go-to-market
**Best for**: Highly aligned businesses
**Example**: Exclusive partnership to serve a specific vertical

## Ideal Partner Profile

### Must Have
- [ ] Similar target audience (but different service)
- [ ] Good reputation and quality work
- [ ] Sufficient client base to generate referrals
- [ ] Responsive and professional communication
- [ ] Win-win mentality

### Nice to Have
- [ ] Existing relationship with us
- [ ] Strong online presence
- [ ] Complementary expertise
- [ ] Shared values and work style
- [ ] Geographic overlap

### Red Flags
- Competitive services (even slightly)
- Poor reputation or reviews
- No clear client base
- All take, no give mentality
- Slow or unprofessional communication

## Partnership Outreach

### Research Phase
Before reaching out:
1. Understand their business model
2. Know their client base
3. Find mutual connections
4. Identify specific synergies
5. Prepare partnership proposal

### Outreach Template

**Subject**: Partnership idea - [Your Company] + [Their Company]

```
Hi [Name],

I lead Ai-Whisperers, and I think there's an interesting partnership opportunity between us.

Here's why:

You work with [type of client] who need [their service]. Many of those same clients struggle with [problem we solve] - and that's exactly what we do.

I'd love to explore how we might:
- Refer clients to each other
- Co-create content for our shared audience
- [Other specific idea]

Would you be open to a 20-minute call to explore?

Either way, I'm a fan of what you're doing with [specific thing they do well].

[Your name]
```

## Partnership Agreement

### What to Define

**Scope**:
- What services are included
- Geographic territories (if any)
- Exclusivity or non-exclusive
- Duration and renewal terms

**Referral Terms**:
- How referrals are made (intro email, warm handoff)
- What information is shared
- Response time expectations
- Feedback on referral outcomes

**Financial Terms** (if applicable):
- Commission/referral fee structure
- When fees are paid
- How to track attribution
- Payment terms

**Co-Marketing** (if applicable):
- Content collaboration plans
- Brand usage guidelines
- Cost sharing for events/ads
- Lead sharing agreements

### Simple Partnership Agreement Template

```
PARTNERSHIP AGREEMENT

Between: [Your Company] and [Partner Company]
Effective Date: [Date]

1. PURPOSE
This partnership aims to [primary goal].

2. REFERRAL PROCESS
- [Your Company] will refer [type of client] to [Partner]
- [Partner] will refer [type of client] to [Your Company]
- Referrals made via [method - email intro, form, etc.]

3. REFERRAL COMPENSATION (if applicable)
- [X]% of first project value for successful referrals
- Paid within 30 days of first client payment
- OR: Reciprocal referrals, no cash exchange

4. CO-MARKETING (if applicable)
- Joint content: [frequency, types]
- Event collaboration: [plans]
- Cost sharing: [terms]

5. CONFIDENTIALITY
Both parties agree to keep client information confidential.

6. TERM
This agreement is effective for [12 months] and renews automatically unless terminated with 30 days notice.

7. EXCLUSIVITY
[Non-exclusive / Exclusive in [territory/vertical]]

Signed:

[Your Company]                    [Partner Company]
Name: _______________            Name: _______________
Date: _______________            Date: _______________
```
