# Thumbnail Guidelines

> Your thumbnail is 90% of whether someone clicks

---

## Why Thumbnails Matter

- YouTube is a visual platform - thumbnails are billboards
- Users decide in 0.5 seconds whether to click
- Good thumbnail + weak title = clicks
- Weak thumbnail + good title = no clicks

---

## Our Thumbnail Style

### Brand Consistency
- **Primary colors**: [Your brand colors]
- **Font**: Bold, sans-serif (Impact, Bebas Neue, Montserrat Black)
- **Style**: Clean, high contrast, professional but energetic

### Elements Every Thumbnail Needs

1. **Face** (for most videos)
   - Clear expression (surprise, excitement, curiosity)
   - Looking at camera or at the text
   - Well-lit, high quality

2. **Text** (3-5 words max)
   - Biggest insight or promise
   - Easy to read on mobile (test at small size)
   - Contrasting colors from background

3. **Visual element**
   - Supporting graphic, icon, or prop
   - Helps communicate the topic instantly
   - Not cluttered - one main element

## Thumbnail Formulas That Work

### Formula 1: Face + Big Text
```
[YOUR FACE]     [BIG TEXT]
(surprised)     "SAVE 10 HRS"
```
Best for: Tutorials, how-to videos

### Formula 2: Before/After
```
[BEFORE]        [AFTER]
(messy/chaos)   (clean/success)
```
Best for: Case studies, transformations

### Formula 3: Comparison
```
[THING A]   VS   [THING B]
(logo)           (logo)
```
Best for: Versus videos, comparisons

### Formula 4: Listicle
```
[NUMBER]    [TOPIC]
  "5"       "AI Tools"
(big)       (visual)
```
Best for: List videos, collections

### Formula 5: Mystery/Curiosity
```
[BLURRED/    [TEXT]
HIDDEN]      "The Secret..."
```
Best for: Thought leadership, reveals

## Text Best Practices

### Do:
- Use 3-5 words maximum
- Make text HUGE (readable on phone)
- Use high contrast colors
- Add outline/shadow for readability
- Test at thumbnail size before finalizing

### Don't:
- Write full sentences
- Use small or thin fonts
- Match text color to background
- Include the full title
- Use more than 2 colors for text

### Power Words That Get Clicks
- FREE
- SECRET
- HACK
- MISTAKE
- STOP
- NEVER
- ALWAYS
- [NUMBER]
- [YEAR]
- TRUTH
- FINALLY

## Face/Expression Guide

| Emotion | When to Use | How to Do It |
|---------|-------------|--------------|
| **Surprised** | Tutorial reveals | Eyes wide, mouth open |
| **Excited** | Positive content | Big smile, raised eyebrows |
| **Serious** | Important topics | Slight frown, direct look |
| **Curious** | Question videos | Head tilt, raised eyebrow |
| **Concerned** | Problem videos | Worried expression |
| **Pointing** | Directing attention | Point at text/element |

### Expression Tips
- Exaggerate more than feels natural
- What looks normal in real life looks flat on screen
- Practice in mirror or review test shots
- Consistent style builds brand recognition

## Technical Specs

- **Size**: 1280 x 720 pixels
- **Format**: JPG or PNG
- **File size**: Under 2MB
- **Safe zone**: Keep text out of bottom right (timestamp overlay)

## Testing Your Thumbnails

### The Phone Test
- View at actual YouTube mobile size
- Can you read everything?
- Does the face stand out?
- Is the topic clear instantly?

### The Blur Test
- Blur the thumbnail 50%
- Can you still understand the topic?
- Do colors and shapes communicate?

### The Scroll Test
- Put thumbnail in a mock YouTube feed
- Does it stand out from surrounding videos?
- Would you click?

---

## A/B Testing Thumbnails

YouTube allows thumbnail testing:
1. Upload video with Thumbnail A
2. After 48 hours, check CTR
3. Change to Thumbnail B
4. After 48 hours, compare CTR
5. Keep the winner

### What to Test
- Different expressions
- Different text
- Different colors
- With/without face
- Different backgrounds
