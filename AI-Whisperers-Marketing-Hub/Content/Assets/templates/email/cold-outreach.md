# Cold Outreach Email Templates

> Proven templates for cold email campaigns

---

## Template 1: The Problem-Focused

**Subject**: Quick question about [their process]

```
Hi [First Name],

I noticed [Company] is [growing/expanding/doing X] - congrats!

Quick question: How is your team handling [specific process]? Most [industry] companies I talk to are spending 10-15 hours/week on this manually.

We help companies like [similar company] automate this - one client cut it from 15 hours to 2.

Worth a 10-minute chat to see if it makes sense for you?

[Your name]
```

**Best for**: When you know a specific pain point

## Template 2: The Case Study Lead

**Subject**: How [Similar Company] saved [result]

```
Hi [First Name],

Thought you'd find this interesting:

[Similar Company in their industry] was spending [X hours/week] on [task]. We automated it and they now spend [Y hours] - plus catch things they used to miss.

Given [Company]'s focus on [their priority], this might be relevant.

Would a brief conversation make sense?

[Your name]
```

**Best for**: When you have a relevant case study

## Template 3: The Trigger Event

**Subject**: Saw the news about [Company]

```
Hi [First Name],

Saw that [Company] just [trigger event - funding, expansion, new product, award].

Companies at this stage often struggle with [related challenge] as they scale.

We help [industry] businesses automate [specific area] so the team can focus on growth instead of admin.
```

## Template 5: The Value-First

**Subject**: [Resource] for [Company]

```
Hi [First Name],

I put together a quick analysis of [their process/industry trend/their website].

[1-2 specific insights or observations]

Would you want me to share the full breakdown? It's about 5 minutes of reading and has some specific ideas for [Company].

No strings attached - just thought it'd be useful.

[Your name]
```

**Best for**: When you can provide genuine value upfront

## Template 6: The Direct Ask

**Subject**: [Specific outcome] for [Company]?

```
Hi [First Name],

I'll be direct: We help [industry] companies automate [process] and typically save 10-15 hours/week.

Wondering if that's something worth a conversation, or should I check back in a few months?

Either answer is fine - just want to respect your time.

```

**Best for**: Decision makers who prefer directness

## Subject Line Variations

**Questions**:
- Quick question about [process]
- How does [Company] handle [task]?
- [Their pain point] at [Company]?

**Results-focused**:
- How [Similar Company] saved [result]
- [X] hours/week back for [Company]?
- Cut [task] time by 80%

**Curiosity**:
- Thought you'd find this interesting
- Idea for [Company]
- Something I noticed about [Company]

**Direct**:
- AI automation for [Company]
- Quick call about [topic]?
- [Outcome] for [Company]?

## Best Practices

### Do:
- Keep it under 100 words
- One clear CTA
- Personalize first line
- Focus on their problem, not your solution
- Test subject lines

### Don't:
- Send attachments in cold emails
- Use "I hope this email finds you well"
- Talk about yourself in the first paragraph
- Use fake personalization
- Send without proofreading

---

## A/B Tests to Run

| Element | Test A | Test B |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Subject | Question format | Statement format |
| Opening | Personalized observation | Direct problem statement |
| CTA | "Quick call?" | "15 minutes this week?" |
| Length | 50 words | 100 words |
| P.S. | With P.S. | Without P.S. |

---

## When to Send

**Best days**: Tuesday - Thursday
**Best times**: 8-10 AM or 2-4 PM (recipient's timezone)
**Avoid**: Monday morning, Friday afternoon
