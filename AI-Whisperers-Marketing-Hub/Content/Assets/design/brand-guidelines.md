# AI-Whisperers Brand Guidelines

> Visual identity standards for consistent brand presentation

---

## Brand Colors

### Primary Palette

| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| **Navy Blue** | `#1a365d` | Primary brand color, headers, CTAs |
| **Orange** | `#ed8936` | Accent, highlights, call-to-action buttons |
| **White** | `#ffffff` | Backgrounds, text on dark |
| **Dark Gray** | `#2d3748` | Body text, secondary text |

### Secondary Palette

| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| **Light Navy** | `#2c5282` | Hover states, secondary buttons |
| **Light Orange** | `#fbd38d` | Backgrounds, highlights |
| **Light Gray** | `#e2e8f0` | Backgrounds, borders |
| **Success Green** | `#38a169` | Success states, positive metrics |

### Usage Guidelines

- **Navy Blue**: Use for headers, important text, primary CTAs
- **Orange**: Use sparingly for emphasis, hover states, key metrics
- **Never**: Use orange for large blocks of text
- **Accessibility**: Ensure 4.5:1 contrast ratio for text

## Typography

### Font Stack

**Primary**: Inter (Google Fonts)
- Clean, professional, highly readable
- Free and widely available

**Fallback**: -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, "Segoe UI", Roboto, sans-serif

### Font Sizes

| Element | Size | Weight | Usage |
|---------|------|--------|-------|
| H1 | 36-48px | 700 (Bold) | Page titles |
| H2 | 28-32px | 600 (Semi-bold) | Section headers |
| H3 | 20-24px | 600 (Semi-bold) | Subsections |
| Body | 16-18px | 400 (Regular) | Paragraph text |
| Small | 14px | 400 (Regular) | Captions, labels |
| Button | 16px | 600 (Semi-bold) | CTAs |

### Line Height

- **Headers**: 1.2 - 1.3
- **Body text**: 1.5 - 1.6
- **Tight (lists)**: 1.4

---

## Logo Usage

### Clear Space

- Maintain minimum clear space equal to the height of the "A" in AI-Whisperers
- Never place text or other elements within this zone

### Minimum Size

- Digital: 120px wide minimum
- Print: 1 inch wide minimum

### Don'ts

- Don't stretch or distort
- Don't add drop shadows or effects
- Don't change colors outside brand palette
- Don't place on busy backgrounds

## Image Guidelines

### Photography Style

- **Tone**: Professional but approachable
- **Subjects**: Real people, real workplaces, technology in use
- **Avoid**: Stock photos with forced smiles, obviously staged scenes
- **Lighting**: Natural, bright, clean

### Graphics & Illustrations

- Clean, minimal design
- Use brand colors consistently
- Icons should be simple, line-based
- Avoid overly complex or busy designs

### Screenshots

- Use actual product/tool screenshots when possible
- Add subtle annotations (arrows, highlights) in brand colors
- Blur sensitive/identifying information

## Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice

| Attribute | What It Means | Example |
|-----------|---------------|---------|
| **Expert** | Knowledgeable, confident | "Here's how to save 20 hours/week..." |
| **Direct** | Clear, no fluff | "Stop wasting time. Automate it." |
| **Practical** | Action-oriented | "Step 1: Identify your most repetitive task" |
| **Human** | Relatable, conversational | "We get it - you're busy" |

### Tone Guidelines

- **LinkedIn Posts**: Professional but conversational
- **Sales Emails**: Helpful, consultative
- **Website Copy**: Clear, benefit-focused
- **Proposals**: Formal but not stiff

### Words We Use

- Automate, streamline, efficiency
- Results, outcomes, ROI
- Partner, collaborate, support
- Simple, straightforward, clear

### Words We Avoid

- Revolutionary, game-changing, disrupt (overused)
- Easy (can feel dismissive of complexity)
- Guarantee (unless specific and documented)
- Cheap (use "affordable" or "cost-effective")
