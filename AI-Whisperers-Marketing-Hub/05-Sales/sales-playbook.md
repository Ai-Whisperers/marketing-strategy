## Philosophy & Mindset

### Our Sales Identity

We are **Challengers**, not Order Takers:
- We teach prospects something new about their business
- We tailor our message to their specific situation
- We take control of the conversation (professionally)

### The Gap Selling Framework

Every sale happens because of a **Gap**:
```
CURRENT STATE ──────► GAP ◄────────── FUTURE STATE
(Pain, Problems)      ▲              (Goals, Dreams)
                      │
              WE SOLVE THE GAP
```

**Our job**: Make the Gap feel HUGE and URGENT

### Chris Voss Negotiation Principles

1. **Tactical Empathy**: Understand their feelings first
2. **Never Split the Difference**: Hold firm on value
3. **Calibrated Questions**: "How" and "What" questions
4. **Silence is Power**: After asking, STOP TALKING
5. **"That's Right" > "Yes"**: Get them to feel understood

### Power Dynamics (Know Before Every Call)

**Our BATNA**: 6-7/10 (We have other prospects, runway, options)
**Their BATNA**: Usually 3-4/10 (Expensive alternatives: DIY tools, consultants, doing nothing)

**Implication**: We can be firm. They need us more than we need them.

## Pre-Call Preparation

### Research Checklist (15 min before call)

**LinkedIn (5 min)**:
- [ ] Current role + tenure
- [ ] Career path (what they did before)
- [ ] Recent posts (what they care about)
- [ ] Mutual connections
- [ ] Company size

**Company (5 min)**:
- [ ] What they do
- [ ] Recent news/announcements
- [ ] Tech stack (if visible)
- [ ] Team size
- [ ] Funding stage (if startup)

**Content (5 min)**:
- [ ] Blog posts they've written
- [ ] Podcasts/interviews
- [ ] Social presence

### BATNA Assessment

Before the call, answer:
1. What's our minimum acceptable deal? **$2,500**
2. What's our walk-away point? **Below $2,500 or scope creep**
3. What are THEIR alternatives? **DIY ($0 but 100+ hours), Consultants ($10K+), Freelancers (variable quality)**

### Set Your Anchor

Based on their profile, pre-determine:
- **Starting anchor**: Usually $5,000-$15,000
- **Target**: Where you want to land
- **Floor**: $2,500 minimum

## Phase 1: Opening (5 min)

### Goal: Build rapport, set agenda, establish control

### Script: The Professional Opening

```
YOU: "[Name], thanks for making time today. Before we dive in,
I want to make sure this is valuable for you regardless of
whether we work together. Sound good?

[Wait for yes]

Great. Here's what I'm thinking: I'd love to spend the first
15 minutes understanding your situation - what's working,
what's not, where you're trying to go. Then I'll share some
observations about what I'm seeing with businesses like yours.
And if it makes sense, we can talk about how we might help.
If not, no pressure - I'll at least give you some ideas you
can use. Fair?

[Wait for yes]

Perfect. To kick us off: what prompted you to take this call
today?"
```

### Key Techniques Used:

1. **"Regardless of whether we work together"** - Reduces defensiveness
2. **Setting agenda** - Establishes your control
3. **"Fair?"** - Gets micro-commitment
4. **Open-ended opener** - Reveals their motivation

### Rapport Builders (Use Research)

```
"I noticed on LinkedIn you've been at [Company] for [X] years -
that's impressive longevity. What's kept you there?"

"I saw your post about [topic] - really resonated with me.
What prompted that?"

"I see you came from [previous role/company]. How's the
transition been to [current role]?"
```

## Phase 2: Discovery - Current State (15 min)

### Goal: Uncover GAP, quantify pain, create urgency

### The Gap Selling Question Framework

#### Level 1: Situation Questions (2-3 min)
*Establish context*

```
"Walk me through a typical week for you. Where does your
time actually go?"

"How many people are on your team? What do they primarily do?"

"What processes feel most manual or repetitive right now?"
```

#### Level 2: Problem Questions (5 min)
*Surface the pain*

```
"What's the biggest challenge you're facing operationally
right now?"

"What tasks do you wish would just... disappear?"

"Where are you losing the most time to things that don't
require your expertise?"

"What keeps falling through the cracks?"
```

#### Level 3: Impact Questions (5 min)
*Make the pain HURT*

```
"How does [problem they mentioned] affect your business?"

"What does this cost you - in time, in money, in stress?"

"What happens if nothing changes in the next 12 months?"

"How is this affecting your ability to grow?"
```

#### Level 4: Root Cause Questions (2-3 min)
*Find the real issue*

```
"What have you tried before to solve this?"

"Why do you think that didn't work?"

"What's preventing you from solving this now?"
```

### Quantifying the Gap (CRITICAL)

**You MUST get numbers**:

```
YOU: "Roughly how many hours per week do you or your team
spend on [task they mentioned]?"

THEM: "Probably 10-15 hours..."

YOU: "Got it. And ballpark - what's the loaded cost of the
person doing this work? Salary plus benefits?"

THEM: "Maybe $30-40 an hour?"

YOU: "So if we're conservative - 10 hours at $35... that's
$350 a week. Over a year, that's about $18,000 just in
direct labor. Not counting the opportunity cost of what
else you could be doing. Does that sound right?"

[Let them agree - now they own the number]
```

### The "That's Right" Summary

After discovery, summarize to trigger "That's Right":

```
YOU: "Let me make sure I understand your situation:

You're spending 15+ hours a week on manual tasks that don't
require your expertise - data entry, follow-ups, reporting.
That's costing you roughly $25,000 a year in direct time,
plus the opportunity cost of not focusing on growth.

You've tried tools like [X] but they required too much
setup and didn't stick. And right now, the problem is
getting worse as you grow - more customers means more
manual work.

The real cost isn't just time - it's the strategic work
that's NOT getting done, and the burnout that's building up.

Did I get that right?"

THEM: "That's right." ← MAGIC WORDS
```

## Phase 3: The Teaching Moment (5 min)

### Goal: Reframe their thinking, establish expertise, create urgency

### Key Stats to Reference (From Research)

Use these data points to build credibility:

| Stat | Value | Use For |
|------|-------|---------|
| AI automation market | $233B → $1.7T by 2032 | "This isn't a trend - it's a transformation" |
| Average automation ROI | 240% | Proving investment value |
| SME segment growth | 32.1% CAGR | "SMEs are adopting fastest" |
| Error reduction | 80-90% | Quality improvement argument |
| Typical time savings | 10-25 hrs/week | Quantifying the opportunity |

### The Challenger Commercial Teaching Pitch

**Structure**: Warmer → Reframe → Rational Drowning → Emotional Impact → New Way → Solution

```
YOU: "[Name], what you're describing is incredibly common.
[WARMER]

Here's what most people don't realize: [REFRAME]

The AI automation market is exploding - $233 billion today,
heading to $1.7 trillion by 2032. The average business owner
spends 40% of their time on tasks that could be automated.
That's 16+ hours a week - over 800 hours a year - doing work
that doesn't require human judgment.

But here's the real problem: [RATIONAL DROWNING]

While you're doing data entry and chasing follow-ups, your
competitors are using AI to do that work in seconds. The
productivity gap is widening. SMEs that adopt automation are
growing 32% faster than those that don't. Every month you
wait, you fall further behind.

And it's not just about efficiency: [EMOTIONAL IMPACT]

The business owners I talk to aren't stressed about tasks -
they're stressed about missing opportunities. Every hour
spent on admin is an hour NOT spent on growth, on
customers, on the work that actually matters to them.

Here's what's changed: [NEW WAY]

AI automation used to require technical teams and six-figure
budgets. Now, we can implement working systems in 30 days
for a fraction of that cost. Our clients see an average ROI
of 240% - that means for every dollar invested, they get
$2.40 back. The businesses thriving in 2025 aren't the
biggest - they're the most automated.

The question isn't whether to automate. [SOLUTION BRIDGE]
It's whether to do it now while you can still catch up,
or later when the gap is even wider.

What's your reaction to that?"
```

### Teach Something They Don't Know

Share an insight that reframes their thinking:

```
"Here's something surprising we've found: most businesses
focus on automating the wrong things. They automate
what's painful instead of what's valuable.

The highest-ROI automations aren't the ones that save
the most time - they're the ones that remove bottlenecks
to growth. Does that resonate with what you're experiencing?"
```

## Phase 4: Future State Vision (5 min)

### Goal: Paint the picture of success, make them want it

### Vision Questions

```
"If we could wave a magic wand, what would your week
look like?"

"What would change for you personally if this problem
was solved?"

"If you had 15 extra hours a week, what would you do
with them?"

"How would your business be different in 12 months
with this in place?"
```

### Paint the Future (Be Specific)

```
YOU: "Let me paint a picture:

It's 3 months from now. You wake up Monday morning,
check your email, and see a summary of everything that
happened over the weekend - leads followed up, data
synced, reports generated. All automatically.

You open your task list and it's just the strategic work -
the stuff that actually moves the needle. No more data
entry. No more chasing follow-ups. No more manual reporting.

Your team is focused on customers instead of spreadsheets.
You're making decisions based on real-time data instead
of gut feel. And that constant low-grade stress of
things falling through cracks? Gone.

That's what automation looks like when it's done right.

How does that sound?"
```

### Contrast Current vs Future

```
YOU: "So right now: 15 hours a week on manual work,
$25,000 a year in direct costs, constant stress about
things slipping.

Future state: Those tasks done in seconds, time back
for strategic work, and the confidence that nothing's
falling through.

Which version do you want to be living in 90 days from now?"
```

## Phase 5: Solution Presentation (10 min)

### Goal: Position our solution as the bridge across their Gap

### Transition from Discovery

```
YOU: "Based on what you've shared, I think we can help.
Let me show you specifically how.

[PAUSE - Don't ask permission, state it confidently]

There are three things we'd focus on for you..."
```

### Present Solutions (Not Features)

**Structure**: Problem → Solution → Outcome

```
YOU: "First, your [specific pain point #1].

Right now, you're spending [X hours] on [task]. We'd
automate that using [approach]. Instead of [current
process], it would work like this: [simple explanation].

The result? That [X hours] becomes [Y minutes], and
you never think about it again.

Second, your [specific pain point #2]..."
```

### Use Their Language

Mirror back their exact words:

```
"You mentioned things 'falling through the cracks' -
here's how we solve that specifically..."

"You said you wish this would 'just disappear' -
that's exactly what automation does..."
```

### Show the ROI

**Industry Benchmark**: Average automation ROI is 240% with 6-9 month payback.
Use this to anchor expectations.

```
YOU: "Let me show you the math on this:

You're currently spending 15 hours/week at roughly
$35/hour loaded cost. That's $27,300 per year.

Our solution typically reduces that to 1-2 hours/week.
Let's say 2 hours - that's $3,640 per year.

Savings: $23,660 per year.

Our Quick Wins package is $5,000 one-time.

That means ROI in the first 90 days, and pure savings
after that. Year one net savings: over $18,000.

Industry average for automation ROI is 240%. You're
looking at 473% return in year one alone.

Does that math make sense to you?"
```

## Phase 6: Pricing & Negotiation

### Goal: Present price confidently, negotiate from strength

### The Price Reveal

**Never apologize for your price. State it as fact.**

```
YOU: "Based on what we've discussed, I'd recommend our
Quick Wins package.

The investment is $5,000.

That includes: implementation of up to 3 automations,
team training, 60 days of support, and our results
guarantee - measurable ROI in 30 days or we keep
working at no additional cost.

[STOP TALKING. Count to 10. Let them respond first.]"
```

### The Ackerman Negotiation Model

If they push back on price:

| Step | Action | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | First offer at 65% of target | They: "$3,500?" |
| 2 | Counter at 85% with empathy | You: "I hear you on budget. $4,250 is the best I can do." |
| 3 | Counter at 95% with trade | You: "If you can commit today, $4,750, and I'll add extra training." |
| 4 | Final at 100% + non-monetary | You: "$5,000 final, but I'll include a quarterly check-in call." |

### Calibrated Questions for Price Pushback

When they say "too expensive":

```
YOU: "Help me understand - too expensive compared to what?"

[Wait for answer - they'll reveal their anchor]

YOU: "And when you think about the $25,000 this is costing
you annually, how does the $5,000 investment feel in that
context?"
```

When they want a discount:

```
YOU: "How am I supposed to deliver the same quality results
at a lower price point?"

[Wait - puts the problem back on them]

YOU: "Here's what I could do: if you can commit today and
provide a testimonial within 60 days, I could do $4,500.
That's the best I can do without reducing scope."
```

### Never Give Without Getting

**Every concession requires a trade:**

| If They Want... | You Get... |
|-----------------|------------|
| Lower price | Faster decision + testimonial |
| Extended payment terms | Longer commitment |
| Extra features | Referral introduction |
| Rush delivery | Premium pricing |

### The Walkaway Price

**Hard floor: $2,500**

If they can't meet $2,500:
```
YOU: "I appreciate you sharing your budget constraints.
At that investment level, we couldn't deliver the results
we'd want for you - and I'd rather be honest about that
than overpromise.

What I can offer is our Starter package at $2,500, which
focuses on one high-impact automation. Once you see the
ROI, we can discuss expanding.

Would that work?"
```

## Phase 7: Objection Handling

### The Voss Method: Label → Mirror → Calibrated Question

### Price Objections

**"It's too expensive"**

```
YOU: "It sounds like budget is a real concern for you."
[LABEL]

THEM: [Elaborates]

YOU: "...a real concern..." [MIRROR - then SILENCE]

THEM: [Reveals more]

YOU: "What would make this feel like the right investment
for you?" [CALIBRATED QUESTION]
```

**"We can't afford it right now"**

```
YOU: "It seems like timing is challenging." [LABEL]

"Help me understand - is it a cash flow issue, or is
there a budget cycle I should know about?"

[Wait for real answer]

"What if we structured payments over 60 days? Would
that change things?"
```

### Timing Objections

**"We need to think about it"**

```
YOU: "Of course - it's an important decision." [ACKNOWLEDGE]

"What specifically would help you decide?" [CALIBRATED]

[Wait - they'll tell you the real objection]

"If we addressed [real concern], would you be ready
to move forward?"
```

**"Let's revisit next quarter"**

```
YOU: "It sounds like there's something specific about
next quarter that makes it better timing." [LABEL]

"Can you help me understand what that is?"

[If brush-off]: "I want to be direct - 'next quarter'
often means 'never' in my experience. If there's a
real concern, I'd rather address it now. What's really
holding you back?"
```

### Trust Objections

**"How do we know this will work?"**

```
YOU: "It sounds like you've been burned before." [LABEL]

"What would give you confidence this is different?"

[Address their specific concern]

"Here's our guarantee: measurable results in 30 days,
or we keep working at no extra cost. We only succeed
if you succeed."
```

**"You're too new / We're your first client"**

```
YOU: "You're right - you'd be one of our early clients.
That's exactly why our pricing is 80% below what
established players charge.

Here's what that means for you: we're highly motivated
to make this a success. We need your testimonial. We'll
over-deliver because we can't afford not to.

And you get zero vendor lock-in - we include source code,
full documentation, everything you need to own this
completely. The risk is actually lower, not higher."
```

### Competition Objections

**"We're looking at other options"**

```
YOU: "Good - you should evaluate alternatives." [ACKNOWLEDGE]

"What criteria matter most in your decision?" [CALIBRATED]

[Position against their criteria]

"How do you feel we compare on those factors?"
```

### The Silent Treatment

After handling any objection:

```
YOU: "Does that address your concern?"

[STOP TALKING. Count to 10. Let them process.]

[If they hesitate]: "It seems like there's something
else on your mind." [LABEL]
```

## Phase 8: Closing (5 min)

### Goal: Get commitment or clear next step

### The Assumptive Close

```
YOU: "Based on everything we've discussed, Quick Wins
at $5,000 seems like the right fit. We'd start with
[specific automation], have it running in 2 weeks, and
you'd see ROI within 30 days.

How does Thursday work for a kickoff call?"
```

### The Summary Close

```
YOU: "Let me summarize where we are:

You're spending $25,000+ annually on manual work that
could be automated. We've identified 3 specific
opportunities that would save you 15 hours a week.

The investment is $5,000, with ROI in 90 days guaranteed.

The only question is timing - do you want to start
getting those hours back now, or later?

What makes sense for you?"

[STOP. WAIT. DO NOT SPEAK FIRST.]
```

### The Direct Ask

If they're hesitant:

```
YOU: "I want to ask you directly: what would it take
for you to say yes today?"

[Wait for answer - address whatever they say]

"If we can solve [that concern], can we move forward?"
```

### If They Need Time

```
YOU: "I understand - take the time you need.

Let's put something on the calendar for [specific date].
That gives you a week to think it through and discuss
internally.

In the meantime, I'll send you [relevant materials].
Is there anything specific that would help you decide?"
```

### The Walk-Away Close (Use Sparingly)

If they're on the fence:

```
YOU: "I want to be honest with you: I can tell you're
not 100% sure about this.

And that's okay. This only works if you're committed
to making it work. If now isn't the right time, I'd
rather we part ways as friends than force something
that doesn't feel right.

Is this a 'not now' or a 'not ever'?"
```

## ICP-Specific Talk Tracks

### ICP 1: Solopreneur ($2,500-$5,000)

**Their World**:
- Wearing all the hats
- Cash-conscious but time-starved
- Want quick wins, not projects

**Opening Hook**:
```
"Most solopreneurs I talk to are doing $100K+ in
revenue but still handling admin like a $50K business.
Is that resonating with you?"
```

**Pain to Emphasize**:
- Time scarcity ("wearing too many hats")
- Fear of complexity ("is this going to be another thing to manage?")
- Opportunity cost ("what could you be doing instead?")

**Value Proposition**:
```
"We help solopreneurs operate like a team of 5 without
the payroll. Most of our solo clients get 10+ hours back
per week within 30 days."
```

**Closing Approach**:
```
"Our Starter package is $2,500. That's probably less than
what you're losing in one month of manual work. And once
it's set up, it runs forever. What questions do you have?"
```

---

### ICP 2: SME Operations Manager ($5,000-$15,000)

**Their World**:
- Team stretched thin
- Pressure from above to "do more with less"
- Need to justify purchases to leadership

**Opening Hook**:
```
"The ops managers I work with are all dealing with the
same thing: growing demand, flat headcount, and processes
that worked at half the size but are breaking now.
Sound familiar?"
```

**Pain to Emphasize**:
- Team burnout
- Process bottlenecks
- Error rates from manual work
- CEO pressure

**Value Proposition**:
```
"We help operations teams scale output without scaling
headcount. Our typical ops client reduces manual work by
40 hours/week across their team - that's like adding a
full-time person without the salary."
```

**Closing Approach** (Help Them Sell Internally):
```
"I know you'll need to bring this to your leadership.
Let me help you build the case: I'll send you a one-pager
with the ROI math and talking points for your CFO.
When do you think you could have that conversation?"
```

---

### ICP 3: Corporate Innovation Lead ($15,000-$50,000+)

**Their World**:
- Need to prove AI/innovation ROI
- Long procurement cycles
- Risk-averse stakeholders
- Looking for quick wins to justify bigger investments

**Opening Hook**:
```
"Most innovation leads I talk to have the same challenge:
the board wants to see AI results, but enterprise solutions
take 6 months just to implement. How are you navigating that?"
```

**Pain to Emphasize**:
- Need to prove value quickly
- Internal credibility at stake
- Competition moving faster
- Procurement friction

**Value Proposition**:
```
"We help innovation teams prove AI ROI in 60 days - not
6 months. Start with a pilot, show results, then scale.
You get the quick win you need, and we grow together."
```

**Closing Approach** (Pilot First):
```
"Here's what I'd suggest: let's start with a Department
package at $15,000 focused on one high-visibility process.
We deliver results in 30 days, you have a success story
for leadership, and then we discuss scaling across other
departments. That reduces your risk and gives you a
proof point. Does that approach make sense?"
```

```
1. OPENING (5 min)
   → Build rapport
   → Set agenda
   → "What prompted this call?"

2. DISCOVERY (15 min)
   → Current state questions
   → Impact questions
   → Quantify the gap
   → Get "That's Right"

3. TEACHING (5 min)
   → Reframe their thinking
   → Share insight they don't know
   → Create urgency

4. FUTURE STATE (5 min)
   → Paint the vision
   → Make them want it

5. SOLUTION (10 min)
   → Bridge the gap
   → Use their language
   → Show ROI math

6. PRICING
   → State confidently
   → STOP TALKING
   → Never give without getting

7. OBJECTIONS
   → Label → Mirror → Calibrate
   → Address, then silence

8. CLOSE
   → Assumptive or direct ask
   → Specific next step
```
