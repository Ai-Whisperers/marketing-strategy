# Discovery Questions

> Uncover pain, budget, timeline, and decision process
> **Methodology**: Gap Selling (quantify current vs future state)
> **Reference**: [sales-playbook.md](../sales-playbook.md) for full scripts

---

## Gap Selling Approach

The goal of discovery is to **quantify the GAP** between where they are and where they want to be. Use these questions to:
1. Understand their **Current State** (pain, problems, costs)
2. Envision their **Future State** (goals, dreams, outcomes)
3. Calculate the **Gap** (what solving this is worth)

## The BANT Framework

- **B**udget - Can they afford it?
- **A**uthority - Can they decide?
- **N**eed - Do they have a problem we solve?
- **T**imeline - When do they need to act?

## Opening Questions

Set the stage for a productive conversation:

1. "Before we dive in, what prompted you to take this call today?"

2. "What do you know about what we do, and what would be helpful to clarify?"

3. "To make sure I'm helpful, can you give me a 2-minute overview of your business?"

4. "What's the #1 thing you'd want to get out of our conversation today?"

---

## Understanding Their Business

### Company Context

5. "How many people are on your team? What do they primarily do?"

6. "What's your business model? How do you make money?"

7. "Who are your typical customers?"

8. "What's your growth trajectory? Where do you want to be in 12 months?"

### Current Operations

9. "Walk me through a typical day/week in your operations."

10. "Where do you and your team spend most of your time?"

11. "What processes feel most manual or repetitive?"

12. "What are the bottlenecks that slow you down?"

---

## Uncovering Pain (Need)

### Surface-Level Pain

13. "What's the biggest challenge you're facing right now?"

14. "What tasks do you wish you could just make disappear?"

15. "Where are you losing the most time to things that don't require your expertise?"

16. "What keeps falling through the cracks?"

### Deeper Pain

17. "How does [problem they mentioned] affect your business?"

18. "What does this cost you in time/money/stress?"

19. "What happens if nothing changes in the next 12 months?"

20. "What have you tried before to solve this?"

### Quantifying Pain

21. "Roughly how many hours per week do you or your team spend on [task]?"

22. "If you could get those hours back, what would you do with them?"

23. "What's the loaded cost of the person doing this work?" (salary + benefits)

24. "Are there errors or issues that come from manual processes? What do those cost?"

---

## Budget Questions

### Direct Budget Questions

25. "Do you have a budget allocated for this type of project?"

26. "What range are you expecting an investment like this to fall into?"

27. "How have you budgeted for similar initiatives in the past?"

### Indirect Budget Questions

28. "What other solutions have you looked at? What were they quoting?"

29. "If we could save you $X per year, what would be a reasonable investment to achieve that?"

30. "Is budget the primary factor, or is timeline/quality more important?"

### Budget Concerns

31. "If budget is tight, would it help to start smaller and expand after seeing results?"

32. "Are there payment terms that would make this easier to approve?"

33. "If this delivered clear ROI in 90 days, would budget be a barrier?"

---

## Authority Questions

### Decision Process

34. "Walk me through how decisions like this typically get made at your company."

35. "Who else would need to be involved in this decision?"

36. "What does [other person] care most about?"

37. "Would it be helpful if I joined a conversation with them?"

### Internal Champions

38. "If you decided to move forward, what would you need to see to recommend this internally?"

39. "What concerns do you think [boss/partner/board] would have?"

40. "Have you made purchases like this before? How did that go?"

---

## Timeline Questions

### Understanding Urgency

41. "Is there a deadline or event driving this?"

42. "When would you ideally want to have this implemented by?"

43. "What happens if this doesn't get done by [date]?"

44. "What's the cost of waiting another 3/6/12 months?"

### Process Timeline

45. "How quickly do you typically move from 'interested' to 'decision made'?"

46. "Are there approval processes that affect timing?"

47. "What would need to happen for you to make a decision by [date]?"

---

## Competition Questions

48. "Are you evaluating other solutions or providers?"

49. "What criteria are most important in your decision?"

50. "What would make you choose one option over another?"

51. "Have you tried solving this before? What happened?"

---

## Vision Questions

Understand what success looks like:

52. "If we could wave a magic wand, what would your operations look like?"

53. "What would change for you personally if this problem was solved?"

54. "How would your business be different in 12 months with this in place?"

55. "What would it mean for your team if we could recover 10 hours per week?"

---

## Closing Discovery Questions

### Summarize Understanding

56. "Let me make sure I understand: [summarize their situation]. Is that accurate?"

57. "Did I miss anything important?"

### Test Fit

58. "Based on what you've shared, I think we can help with X, Y, Z. Does that align with what you're looking for?"

59. "On a scale of 1-10, how important is solving this problem in the next 90 days?"

### Next Steps

60. "What would be most helpful as a next step?"

61. "Would it make sense for me to put together a proposal based on what we discussed?"

62. "What questions do you have for me?"

---

## Question Flow

### Recommended Sequence

1. **Opening** (5 min): Set context, build rapport
2. **Business Context** (5 min): Understand their world
3. **Pain Discovery** (10 min): Find and quantify problems
4. **Vision** (5 min): Understand their goals
5. **Budget/Authority/Timeline** (5 min): Qualify the opportunity
6. **Wrap-Up** (5 min): Summarize, next steps

### Total Time: 30-45 minutes

---

## Questions to Avoid

- Leading questions that put words in their mouth
- Multiple questions at once (ask one at a time)
- Yes/no questions when you want detail
- Questions you should already know the answer to
- Questions that feel like interrogation

---

## Active Listening

### Follow-Up Prompts

- "Tell me more about that."
- "What do you mean by [term they used]?"
- "How did that affect you?"
- "What happened next?"
- "That's interesting - why do you think that is?"

### Summarizing

- "So if I'm hearing you correctly..."
- "It sounds like the main issue is..."
- "Let me make sure I understand..."

---

## Discovery Call Checklist

Before ending the call, confirm:

- [ ] Clear understanding of their business
- [ ] Specific pain points identified and quantified
- [ ] Budget range discussed or understood
- [ ] Decision makers identified
- [ ] Timeline established
- [ ] Competition/alternatives known
- [ ] Next steps agreed
- [ ] Follow-up scheduled
