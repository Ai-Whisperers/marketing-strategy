## Discovery Questions to Ask

**Expose the time cost:**
- "How many courses have you bought that you never finished?"
- "If you spent 100 hours learning this, what business activities wouldn't get done?"
- "What's your hourly rate? Let's calculate the real cost of learning it yourself."

**Expose the risk:**
- "What happens if you get stuck halfway through?"
- "Have you implemented anything from courses before? How did that go?"
- "What's your backup plan if the DIY approach doesn't work?"

**Expose the opportunity cost:**
- "How much is the problem costing you per month right now?"
- "If you could solve this quickly vs taking 6 months, what's that worth?"
- "What else could you accomplish with 100 extra hours?"

## Objection Handling

### "I like learning new things"

```
"I love that about you - continuous learning is a founder superpower.

Here's my take: There are skills worth learning yourself, and skills worth outsourcing.

Building your core product? Worth learning.
Understanding your customers? Worth learning.
Setting up a Zapier automation? Maybe not the best use of your learning time.

The goal isn't to never learn automation - it's to learn by USING it, not by watching videos about it. We build it, you use it, and you naturally learn how it works through real usage.

You'll be more competent with automation after 30 days of using our systems than after 100 hours of courses."
```

### "I've already bought a course"

```
"How far did you get with it?

[If they didn't finish]: That's incredibly common. The completion rate for online courses is about 10-15%. Not because people aren't smart - because life gets in the way.

Here's an option: Let us implement what you've been trying to learn. You'll see it working in your actual business, which is better learning than any video.

And honestly? The course probably taught concepts we can build on. Your learning wasn't wasted - it just needs execution now."
```

### "It's so much cheaper"

```
"On paper, absolutely. $50 vs $5,000 is a big difference.

But let's do the real math:

Course: $50 + 100 hours of your time + 3-6 months to results
If your time is worth $75/hour, that's $7,500 + $50 = $7,550 real cost
Plus the opportunity cost of 6 months of delayed savings.

Us: Hourly/fixed rate + 10 hours of your time + fast turnaround
Real cost: Fraction of DIY when you value your time
Plus you start saving immediately.

The 'expensive' option is actually cheaper when you value your time.

And there's one more factor: If the course doesn't work out, you've lost 100 hours and still have the problem. If we don't deliver, we keep working until you get results - that's our guarantee."
```

### "I want to understand it myself so I'm not dependent on vendors"

```
"That's smart thinking - I'd want the same thing.

Here's how we address that:

1. We document everything we build - you get the 'how' and 'why'
2. We include training - your team learns to maintain and modify
3. No vendor lock-in - you own the systems, not us
4. We use standard tools - nothing proprietary or secret

You'll actually understand it BETTER by using a working system than by watching tutorials about hypothetical scenarios.

It's like learning to drive: You can watch YouTube videos, or you can get in a car with an instructor. Which one actually makes you a driver?

And our promise: It will work. And it won't take forever."
```

## When to Walk Away

If they're dead-set on DIY:

```
"I respect that decision. Here's what I'd suggest:

Start with the course. Give it an honest 30 days. If you get stuck or decide your time is better spent elsewhere, reach out.

No pressure - the door's always open. Would it be okay if I followed up in a month to see how it's going?"
```

**Then**: Add to 90-day nurture sequence. They'll likely be back.
